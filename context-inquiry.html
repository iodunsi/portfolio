Understanding Player Decision Making In Animal Crossing

This study explored video game playing habits and decision-making, with a focus on Animal Crossing: New Horizons. Through contextual inquiry, I observed a participant’s gameplay session to understand how they navigate game mechanics, prioritize tasks, and engage with different features.

View Report

Contextual Inquiry Process

Participant & Context

Observed my sister, an experienced gamer, during a 1-hour Animal Crossing session.

Data Collection

Recorded gameplay, used Otter.ai for transcription, asked semi-structured questions throughout.

Key Insights

Identified how players prioritize tasks, interact with mechanics, and the role of music in immersion.

UX Artifacts

Storyboard

Visualized a player’s journey and decision-making flow during gameplay.

Empathy Map

Mapped player thoughts, feelings, and behaviors to highlight motivations and frustrations.

Persona

Created a representative persona summarizing key player traits, goals, and pain points.

Note: The enlarged version of these artifacts are linked in the report above for reference.

Reflection

This project was very fun to complete and it was very insightful in regards to discovering user needs and how gameplay elements such as soundtracks add to the user's overall experience. I had never conducted this type of study before nor heard of this method, so it was interesting going through the process. It was also interesting to see pain points in user experience show up in a video game, rather than a website or an app.
